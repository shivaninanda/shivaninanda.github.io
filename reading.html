TikTok Ban: The Security Fix That Fixes Nothing
January 7, 2025

Banning TikTok might seem like a bold move, but it won’t solve the larger data-security problem. While concerns about ByteDance’s ties to the Chinese government are valid, Google and Meta collect far more data on Americans than TikTok ever has. The real issue lies in the broader ecosystem of tech companies and data brokers trading personal information without adequate regulation. Misinformation and propaganda are also cited as reasons for a ban, but these are problems across all social media platforms, not just TikTok. Instead of focusing on one app, Congress should prioritize comprehensive digital privacy laws to address the root of the issue. Targeting TikTok alone feels more like a distraction than a solution.

Jan Hatzius on 2025: Balancing Growth, Risks, and Holiday Cheer
December 22, 2024

Jan Hatzius, Goldman Sachs’ chief economist, is optimistic about 2025, projecting steady growth of 2.5% despite challenges like increased tariffs and reduced immigration. He emphasizes that strong income gains and easing inflation will help the economy remain on track, even as policy changes create uncertainty. His analysis underscores the balance between risks and resilience in today’s economic landscape. In a lighter moment, Hatzius joked about the inefficiency of holiday gifting, blending humor with his usual sharp insights. His thoughtful take on what lies ahead offers both clarity and perspective as we enter the new year.

Macy’s Probe Found Employee Acted Alone in $151 Million Accounting Scandal
December 11, 2024

Macy’s recent $151 million accounting scandal is a reminder of how even one unchecked mistake can spiral out of control. An employee’s error in tracking delivery expenses, followed by years of falsified records, delayed financial reports and shook investor confidence. The fallout has forced Macy’s to lower its profit expectations and focus on damage control, like strengthening internal systems and doubling down on its top-performing stores. It’s a tough lesson in accountability and oversight, and it raises questions about how such a large-scale issue went unnoticed for so long—even by the company’s auditor.

The Secrets of the Man Who Made Nvidia the World’s Most Valuable Company
December 10, 2024

Jensen Huang, Nvidia’s CEO, has a unique way of staying connected to every corner of his company: T5T emails. These "Top-5 Things" updates from employees help him cut through bureaucracy and spot trends early, like the AI boom that propelled Nvidia’s rise. His relentless work ethic and focus on ground-level insights have created a culture of transparency and agility, making the company one of the most innovative in the world. It’s a simple yet powerful reminder that sometimes the best ideas come from listening to everyone, not just the top executives.

Banks are increasingly hiring tech talent for projects in AI and machine learning, focusing on innovation and research.
